Ehteraam (احترام), derived from Arabic, means
respect, honor, reverence, and paying attention. It is a masculine noun frequently used in Urdu and Hindi to denote holding someone or something in high regard, deference, or veneration. Common phrases include qaabil-e-ehtiraam (venerable/honorable) and ehtiraaman (respectfully). 
Key aspects of Ehteraam: 
  • Definition: Deep respect, honoring, deference, veneration, and esteem.
  • Usage: It refers to showing honor to individuals, feelings, or relationships (e.g., ehtiraam-e-ishq - respect of love).
  • Context: It is often used to describe holding someone in high regard or showing deference
